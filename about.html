a little bit about me.


My name is Vivian Yuan, and I am an undergraduate junior double majoring in Human-Computer Interaction and Business Administration at Carnegie Mellon University. I first heard about HCI and UI/UX Design in my freshman spring, and since then, I have started noticing so many things in my everyday life that could be improved with design, ranging from my school’s internal sites all the way to my toothpaste tubes. I love being able to work on and see transformations in digital products from ideation to development, and it is so fulfilling to be able to see the tangible effects of good design. I am amazed by how far we’ve progressed in the field, and I am excited to be a part of great things to come.

In my free time, I love to play games with my friends, whether it be tactical first-person shooter games like Valorant, or fast-paced strategy board games like One Night Werewolf. I also grew up singing in a choir and have continued to sing in a cappella groups since my freshman year of high school. I played the flute from fourth grade to senior year of high school, participating in different state and honors orchestras and bands throughout the years. UI/UX design is a perfect way for me to combine the critical thinking skills I gained from gaming with the creativity that I have nurtured since elementary school, and I am so glad to have found a field that I am passionate about.
